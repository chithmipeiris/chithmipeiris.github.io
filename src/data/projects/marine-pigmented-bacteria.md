## Overview

This is my ongoing MPhil research, carried out as a Graduate Research Assistant in the
Department of Botany, University of Sri Jayewardenepura, under the supervision of
Dr. Isuru U. Kariyawasam. The project investigates marine pigmented bacteria associated
with the phycoplane of brown seaweeds (*Padina* sp.) for their UV-blocking and antioxidant
properties.

## Motivation

Marine microbes are a rich, underexplored source of bioactive natural products. Pigments
produced by seaweed-associated bacteria may offer natural, sustainable alternatives to
synthetic UV filters and antioxidants — with applications in cosmetics, biomedicine and
industry.

## Approach

- Isolation, screening and cultivation of marine pigmented bacterial strains.
- Optimisation of fermentation parameters for bacterial pigment production.
- Pigment extraction, purification and analysis using chromatographic techniques.
- Antioxidant assays (DPPH, ABTS and FRAP) to determine bioactive potential.
- Molecular characterisation of isolates via DNA extraction and 16S rRNA PCR amplification.

## Highlights

Pigmented isolates including *Brevibacterium* sp. and *Micrococcus* sp. — recovered from
*Padina* sp. collected in Hikkaduwa, Sri Lanka — have shown promising antioxidant and
UV-protective activity, and have been presented at international marine science conferences.

![Fieldwork on the southern coast of Sri Lanka](/images/projects/marine-pigmented-bacteria/fieldwork.jpg)

## What's Next

Further structural characterisation of the pigments, scale-up of fermentation, and
evaluation of their performance in real-world UV-protective and antioxidant applications.
