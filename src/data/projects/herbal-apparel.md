## Overview

An independent research-based undergraduate assignment (2023–2024) in the Department of
Plant Sciences, University of Colombo, supervised by Dr. Jinendra Dissanayake.

## Focus

The study examined trends in the production and consumption of herbal apparel — textiles
treated with medicinal and aromatic plant extracts — in the Sri Lankan context, exploring
its growth as a sustainable and health-oriented segment of the textile industry.

## Why It Matters

Herbal apparel sits at the intersection of plant science, sustainability and consumer
markets. Understanding its production and demand helps identify where botanical innovation
can create value while reducing the environmental footprint of conventional textiles.
