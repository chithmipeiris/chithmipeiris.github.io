**Mapping the Innovation Landscape of Herbal Apparel in Sri Lanka: Consumer Perceptions, Sustainable Production Systems, and Future Research Opportunities.**

*Independent Research-Based Undergraduate Assignment · Department of Plant Sciences, University of Colombo · 2023 – 2024*

## Overview

As global fashion industries transition toward sustainability-driven production systems,
herbal apparel has emerged as a novel intersection between traditional botanical knowledge,
textile engineering, wellness products, and eco-conscious consumerism. This project
investigated the emerging herbal apparel sector in Sri Lanka through a multidisciplinary
assessment of production practices, consumer behaviour, industry challenges, and future
innovation opportunities. By integrating consumer surveys, field-based industry
observations, stakeholder interviews, and expert consultations, the study developed one of
the first comprehensive assessments of Sri Lanka's herbal apparel ecosystem. The research
identified critical barriers to industry expansion, evaluated market readiness, and
highlighted technological opportunities capable of transforming herbal apparel from a niche
product into a globally competitive sustainable textile sector.

## Background

Despite Sri Lanka's rich biodiversity, strong textile manufacturing infrastructure, and
centuries-old traditions of natural dyeing, limited research has examined the commercial
viability, consumer acceptance, and production challenges associated with herbal apparel.
Existing knowledge remained fragmented across production systems, consumer behaviour, and
technological development. This project addressed these knowledge gaps by systematically
investigating the entire herbal apparel value chain, from raw material sourcing and
manufacturing practices to consumer perception and future technological innovation.
Particular emphasis was placed on identifying research and development priorities required
to support the growth of Sri Lanka's sustainable fashion industry.

## Methods

A mixed-methods research framework was developed to evaluate both the production and
consumption dimensions of the herbal apparel industry. Consumer perception and market
readiness were assessed through a nationwide survey involving randomly distributed
participants representing diverse demographic backgrounds. The survey investigated awareness
levels, purchasing behaviour, perceived health benefits, sustainability perceptions, price
sensitivity, and future purchasing intentions.

To understand industrial production systems, field visits were conducted at leading herbal
apparel manufacturers and sustainable textile companies across Sri Lanka, including
production facilities, retail operations, and industry stakeholders. Structured interviews
were performed with manufacturers, managers, and industry practitioners to document
production workflows, sustainability practices, sourcing strategies, market trends, and
operational challenges. Future development pathways were investigated through consultations
with textile engineers, fashion technology specialists, sustainable apparel experts, and
academic researchers. Information from literature reviews, stakeholder interviews, field
observations, and consumer surveys was integrated to generate a comprehensive innovation
landscape assessment of the herbal apparel sector.

## Results

The study revealed a rapidly emerging yet underdeveloped herbal apparel industry with
significant growth potential in both domestic and international markets. Consumer analysis
demonstrated a substantial awareness gap, with **42.2%** of respondents unfamiliar with
herbal apparel products, indicating that market expansion remains constrained primarily by
education and public engagement rather than product acceptance. Despite this limited
awareness, consumers who had previously used herbal apparel reported positive experiences
associated with comfort, relaxation, improved sleep quality, and perceived wellness
benefits.

Importantly, the study identified strong market readiness for future adoption. All surveyed
participants expressed willingness to consider herbal apparel purchases in the future, while
**73.4%** indicated willingness to pay a premium price for products supported by scientific
evidence, sustainability credentials, and demonstrable health-related benefits.

Field investigations revealed that Sri Lankan herbal apparel manufacturers have successfully
developed highly sustainable production systems utilising plant-derived dyes, biodegradable
processing methods, natural mordants, circular waste management practices, and village-based
production economies. The study documented how manufacturing residues are recycled into
biofertilisers and agricultural inputs, creating environmentally and socially sustainable
production networks.

A critical finding of the research was the identification of a major technological gap
within the industry. While current production systems successfully utilise traditional
herbal dyeing approaches, advanced textile technologies such as microencapsulation,
controlled-release systems, sensory textiles, and biofunctional finishing technologies
remain largely absent from Sri Lankan production pipelines. These technologies were
identified as key innovation opportunities capable of significantly enhancing product
functionality, international competitiveness, and export potential. Collectively, the project
established a strategic roadmap for advancing Sri Lanka's herbal apparel sector through
scientific validation, technological innovation, consumer education, and sustainable
manufacturing development.

## Scientific & Industry Contributions

- Conducted one of the first comprehensive assessments of Sri Lanka's emerging herbal
  apparel industry.
- Evaluated consumer awareness, purchasing behaviour, and market readiness for sustainable
  biofunctional textiles.
- Mapped the complete herbal apparel value chain through field-based industry
  investigations.
- Identified key production bottlenecks affecting scalability, market penetration, and
  international competitiveness.
- Assessed sustainability practices within herbal apparel manufacturing systems.
- Recognised advanced textile technologies, including microencapsulation and
  controlled-release finishing systems, as future innovation opportunities.
- Developed evidence-based recommendations for industry expansion, consumer education, and
  technology adoption.
