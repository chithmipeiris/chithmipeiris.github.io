## Overview

My undergraduate research project in the Department of Plant Sciences, University of
Colombo (2023–2024), supervised by Dr. Jinendra Dissanayake, Dr. Sameera Ariyawansa and
Prof. M.N. Kaumal. The project explored fungal pigments as sustainable, bio-based
alternatives to synthetic dyes.

## Background

The synthetic dye industry carries a significant environmental burden. Pigment-producing
fungi offer a renewable route to natural colourants — but realising this potential
requires identifying suitable strains and characterising their pigments.

## Methods

- Screened previously preserved fungal isolates from lichen (*Rocella montagne*) samples
  collected at the Kalpitiya Lagoon, Sri Lanka, to identify pigment-producing species
  (*Talaromyces verruculosus*).
- Optimised submerged liquid-state fermentation conditions.
- Extracted pigments using ethyl acetate and purified them by Thin Layer Chromatography (TLC).
- Characterised the compounds using UV-Vis, FTIR and NMR.

## Results

The red pigment was identified as **austrocortirubin**, an anthraquinone polyketide —
contributing a promising, sustainable alternative to synthetic dyes.

## Outputs

This work has been developed into a book chapter and a preprint article on sustainable
fungal-derived dyes, and presented at the International Forestry and Environment Symposium.
