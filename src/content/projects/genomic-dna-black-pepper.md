## Overview

An undergraduate group research project (May–November 2022) in the Department of Plant
Sciences, University of Colombo, supervised by Prof. H.D. Dharshani Bandupriya and
Dr. Anushka Wikramasuriya.

## The Challenge

Black pepper (*Piper nigrum* L.) leaves are rich in polysaccharides and secondary
metabolites that co-precipitate with DNA and inhibit downstream molecular applications.
Reliable, high-quality genomic DNA is essential for PCR, sequencing and marker work.

## Approach

The project systematically evaluated and compared multiple DNA extraction protocols,
assessing yield and purity to identify the most effective method for this challenging
plant tissue.

## Outcome

The comparison identified extraction conditions that consistently deliver high-quality
genomic DNA suitable for molecular analyses. The findings were presented at the 43rd
Annual Sessions of the Institute of Biology, Sri Lanka.
